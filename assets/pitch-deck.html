Slide 02 · of 10 · Problem + Market
Every bank using AI has no way to prove their model is safe. SR 11-7 requires independent validation. Nobody sells it at scale.

The problem. SR 11-7 (Federal Reserve + OCC) mandates independent model validation for every AI system used in lending, fraud detection, AML, trading, and risk scoring. OCC Bulletin 2026-13 just updated the guidance. FINRA's 2026 Oversight Report puts GenAI at the top of the compliance priority list. Every bank, every fintech, every insurer deploying AI needs third-party validation — and they're paying Big-4 firms $100K+ per model, waiting 6 months per audit.

Why now. OCC 2026-13 just dropped. FINRA says "move from experimentation to disciplined implementation." EU AI Act conformity deadlines Aug 2026. GAO published GAO-25-107197 documenting the oversight gap. When GAO writes a report, regulation follows. The compliance cliff is here.

TAM: $1B-$2B, grounded in existing cert markets.

  • SOC 2 certification market: ~$1.5B. Every SaaS company buys one. AI model validation is the next SOC 2.
  • PCI QSA market: ~$2B. Every company processing payments buys one. AI models in payments need the same.
  • 8,000+ US banks deploying 5-50 AI models each = 40K-400K models needing validation
  • Add fintech, insurance, asset management = 2-3x multiplier on banking alone

Origin: CSAM detection. We started by certifying AI image models are free of child sexual abuse material — the hardest, most legally sensitive audit in AI. The same 5-gate pipeline that detects CSAM in training data detects bias in lending models, PII memorization in chatbots, and backdoors in trading algorithms. CSAM is the moral anchor. Finance is the market.

Slide 04 · of 10 · Underlying Magic
The moat: hardware root of trust + reproducible five-gate pipeline. Both required. Neither copied easily.

1. Air-gapped hardware signing. Private key burned into ESP32 eFuse silicon inside a bolted-down safe. Biometric touch to issue a cert. No cloud. No API. No extraction path that doesn't involve physical access to the operator's safe and finger.

2. Five-gate audit pipeline. Hash scan → red-team battery → membership inference → dataset attestation → harmful-output classifier. Each gate emits artifacts. Artifacts hash into the signature. A skeptic can re-run every gate and verify against the published pubkey.

3. Reproducibility is the product. A cert you can re-verify is a cert a court, a marketplace, and a regulator can all accept. A SaaS black-box attestation is not.

4. The radioactive data problem. Real CSAM data is federal evidence. It cannot be stored, shared, demonstrated, or used in a PoC. Every other company in this space hits this wall. We solved it with the Exodia Architecture: the entire 5-gate pipeline is scaffolded on safe 32x32 pixel art (skeletons = flagged content, knights = clean content). Same code paths, same gate logic, zero harmful content in the repo. Swap to production by changing three things: hash DB source (NCMEC), classifier weights (retrained), one environment variable. The code does not change. The PoC is live and testable TODAY without legal, ethical, or storage risk.

Platform note: 8 Rust crates — multiple specialized systems, each handling one function, governed by a compositor that selects which module to activate. Same architectural diversity principle as multi-system AGI: hash library, inference runner, gate logic, signing tool, job queue, report pipeline. Only the compositor assembles the full audit system. No single crate can be misused, forked, or weaponized alone. Named after Yu-Gi-Oh Exodia: five useless cards that win the game when assembled. Slide 06 · of 10 · Go-to-Market
The Docker Hub model for AI compliance. Push your model. Get certified. Pass the exam.

The Docker Hub analogy. Docker Hub became the default registry because every container got scanned, signed, and badged before it shipped. KNOXAI is the same model for AI: publishers push a model, operators audit it through 5 gates, a hardware-signed cert ships back. Marketplaces display the badge. Regulators accept the cert. Uncerted models become unshippable — just like unsigned Docker images became undeployable in enterprise.

This is a Lesse's Law moment. Three regulatory deadlines converge in 2026 — EU AI Act, DoD Section 1513, UK OSA AI expansion. This is not incremental growth. This is a discontinuous leap in compliance demand. The market jumps, not curves. We are positioned before the jump.
