How We Team

Two tracks. The first is your contract; we slot in. The second is our small business; you team with us as the prime or as the sub for transition. Pick whichever makes the procurement office happy.

As Prime · Teaming Preferred

You team with us; we hold the small business set-aside.

  • SBIR Phase I — concept feasibility; subs allowed up to 33% SDVOSB set-aside topics; AFWERX, SpaceWERX, NavalX, DARPA, NIH, NSF Open Topics
  • SBIR Phase II — prototype build; subs allowed up to 50% Cost-reimbursement-aware; up to ~$2M per topic
  • SBIR Phase III — sole-source, no dollar cap Team with us as the transition vehicle; we sub UNDER you for delivery
  • STTR — small biz + research-institution partner Open to UMD, JHU APL, MITRE-affiliated researcher pairings
  • Direct-to-Phase-II (D2P2) 32-crate Unlicense base + IR&D openbooks = Phase I-equivalent evidence
  • SDVOSB Joint Venture JV with another SDVOSB to combine past performance for Phase II / set-aside pursuits
  • SBA / DoD Mentor-Protégé Open to 18-month mentor-protégé arrangements; primes can sub to us on set-asides without disqualifying SDVOSB status
  • STRATFI / TACFI — post-Phase II match funding Commercial revenue (KNOXAI + oakilydokily + consulting) provides the private match
Best when you've got a topic, a research lead, a Phase III transition need — or you want to mentor.

Architecture Taught Under The Unlicense

When we team, we don't gatekeep. The architecture is the deliverable. The architecture is public domain. Bring the contract; the patterns travel under the Unlicense.

  • Single-binary Rust web service pattern — this site is the reference impl. 10 MB binary, $10/mo bare-metal hosting, zero cloud. Source on GitHub, Unlicense.
  • Triple-Sims testing gate — three-stage simulation before merge. Implemented in exopack. Re-used across every Cochran Block crate.
  • Edge / DIL / disconnected deployment patterns — operates in disconnected, intermittent, limited-bandwidth environments. Zero external dependencies at runtime.
  • On-device AI inference — any-gpu, pixel-forge, kova-engine. Runs on consumer hardware. No FedRAMP cloud auth required because there is no cloud.
  • Distributed C2 mesh — ghost-fabric, runsible. Multi-node orchestration via SSH with tokenized command compression. Resync on connectivity restore.
  • Wall-of-Laptops TUI / framebuffer-console pattern — zero-GUI status displays. ~80 MB RAM total per node. Repurpose, don't replace.
  • The cochranblock crate itself — a template for instantiating your own anti-founder website. Fork it, rename it, point it at your own catalog. Public domain.
  • Custom AI orchestration over curated, research-backed datasets — multiple shipped instances. Data is the moat, not the model. Researched and curated corpora, paired with on-device inference, deliver the kind of dual-use AI that’s defensible without an OpenAI bill or a FedRAMP cloud auth.
No license fee. No NDA needed for the patterns. Full crate-by-crate breakdown at manual.cochranblock.org.
